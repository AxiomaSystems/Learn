# Goals

## Product thesis

Axioma Learn should become a unified learning workspace where students can progress with confidence and educators can teach with better visibility, less admin friction, and context-aware AI support.

## Primary goals

### 1. Reduce learning friction for students

Students should not have to search across tabs, tools, and threads to understand:

- what is due
- what to study next
- where the materials are
- how they are doing
- how to ask for help

The product should turn ambiguity into clear next actions.

### 2. Reduce operational overhead for educators

Educators should be able to:

- see class health quickly
- identify students who need support early
- review coursework efficiently
- send targeted class or student communication
- manage office hours and task flow from one place

The product should cut repetitive coordination work and surface meaningful teaching decisions.

### 3. Make AI genuinely useful through context

AI is not a decorative chatbot. It should be grounded in:

- the current class
- the active coursework item
- class resources
- student progress
- grade patterns
- scheduling context

Every AI interaction should be role-aware, explainable, and tied to a concrete action or outcome.

### 4. Unify fragmented academic workflows

The final product should unify capabilities that are often spread across LMS tools, shared docs, messaging, calendars, and scheduling tools:

- coursework
- resources
- submissions
- messaging
- grades
- office hours
- planning
- class setup

### 5. Create a calm, supportive product tone

The prototype makes it clear that the intended final product is not a stressful dashboard. It should feel:

- warm
- legible
- reassuring
- focused
- actionable

This matters especially for students who are overwhelmed and educators who are overloaded.

## User goals

### Student goals

- Know what to do today.
- Understand each assignment without hunting for context.
- See all class materials in one place.
- Ask for help without feeling lost or embarrassed.
- Book time with instructors easily.
- Receive study guidance that feels specific, not generic.

### Educator goals

- Understand how a class is doing at a glance.
- Detect weak understanding patterns early.
- Help the right students at the right time.
- Review and communicate faster.
- Create class structure with less setup work.
- Use AI as an assistant for judgment, not a replacement for judgment.

## Business and product goals

- Increase student engagement and completion.
- Increase educator efficiency and course clarity.
- Improve retention by making the product part of daily workflow.
- Differentiate through role-aware, context-grounded AI.
- Build a platform that can support multiple course models and institutions.

## Non-goals

The final product should avoid becoming:

- a generic chatbot wrapped in education branding
- a file dump with prettier UI
- an LMS clone that only rearranges existing complexity
- an analytics-heavy tool that lacks actionability
- an educator-only product that neglects student experience

## Success indicators

Examples of success metrics for the final version:

- Students open fewer screens before starting work.
- Students complete more tasks on time.
- Students book help earlier when they are stuck.
- Educators spend less time locating context for grading and outreach.
- Educators send fewer repeated clarifications because the system centralizes context.
- AI actions lead to measurable follow-through, not just chat usage.
